My Catholic Perspective

As a Catholic who actively attends Mass, I realise we must work hard to preserve what Pope Benedict XVI calls the Earth, “Our Common Home.” Climate change is an issue ignored for too long, and we cannot let it continue.

God says in the Bible, “In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth” (Genesis 1:1). As caretakers of creation, we must respect His gift and nurture the environment. In Aotearoa New Zealand, I’m influenced by Māori values that treat the natural world, land, sea, and sky, as sacred, deserving deep respect and care.

In 2015, Pope Francis released his encyclical Laudato Si’, calling all people to respond to “the cry of the Earth and the cry of the poor.” He recognised that climate change is a moral issue, the poorest suffer the most despite contributing the least.

This teaching highlights that caring for the environment is an act of love. But just any love, love for God, for our neighbours, and for future generations. To ignore the damage we cause is to neglect God’s creation. As Catholics, we are called to live simply, reduce waste, and advocate for policies that protect both the planet and its people.

From my perspective, responding to climate change is more than environmental activism, it is an act of faith. By protecting the Earth, we protect life in all its forms and honour the Creator who entrusted it to us.

This website stands for that faith in action, calling for change, compassion, and hope for our Common Home.
